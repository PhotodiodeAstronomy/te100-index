Every Purchase is a Transformation

When you spend $1 at Walmart, that dollar doesn’t vanish.
It transforms into:

  • ✅ Groceries on your table
  • ✅ Wages for 2.1 million workers
  • ✅ Trucks, warehouses, solar panels
  • ✅ And yes — a tiny profit (2.4¢)

Transformation Efficiency (TE) asks:
“How much of your $1 became real stuff — vs. profit?”

Walmart: 97.6¢ → real value | 2.4¢ → profit → TE = 97.6%
Uber: You pay $1 → get $1.05 in ride (thanks to investor funding) → TE = 105%

CTE: Only Your Dollar Counts

Not all revenue comes from you.
Amazon makes $190B from AWS (businesses), not shoppers.

How Big Companies Lower Your Prices

1900: A loaf of bread cost 5 days of wages.
2025: 5 minutes.

Why? Economies of Scale — the magic of getting better at making more.

  • Walmart buys 1 billion bananas → pays less → you pay $0.58/lb
  • Amazon ships 7.5B packages → builds robots → Prime = $139/year
  • Costco pays workers $25/hr → turnover 6% → you save $1,200/year

Scale ≠ greed. Scale = lower prices.

Corporations Don’t Harm Consumers — They Serve Them

The old story: “Big = bad. Profit = theft.”
The new story: Big = efficient. Profit = incentive.

“If you hate corporations, delete Amazon, Walmart, and Uber from your life.
You’ll pay 40% more, wait 3 days for delivery, and walk to work.”

TE proves it: High TE = Low prices = Consumer wins.

  • ✅ Walmart: 97.6% TE → lowest grocery prices in history
  • ✅ Amazon: 90.8% CTE → 2-day shipping for $139/year
  • ✅ Uber: 105% TE → you pay $1, get $1.05 in ride

Econ 101 + 10-K Rigor

TE = 100 − Net Profit Margin
CTE = 100 − (Net Income / Consumer Revenue)

Derived from SEC 10-K filings (us-gaap:Revenues, NetIncomeLoss).
Unlike consumer surplus (subjective), TE/CTE is objective, audited, real-time.

Implication: Minimum efficient scale (MES) → ↓ ATC → ↑ TE → ↓ P → ↑ consumer welfare.
